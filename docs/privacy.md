# Privacy Policy for WhatNow? Kids

**Last updated:** 2026-04-17

WhatNow? Kids ("the app", "we") is built and operated by Parenting AI. This policy explains what data the app collects, why, and what you can do about it. We tried to keep it short and honest.

## What we collect

The app collects two narrow categories of data, both anonymous:

**1. Anonymous usage events** — when you open the app, complete a spin, open the Customize Wheel screen, or hit an empty filter combination. For spin events we also record which filters were active (time / location / energy). We use this to understand which features get used and which don't.

**2. Crash and performance data** — if the app crashes or slows down, we receive a technical report (stack trace, device model, iOS version). We use this to fix bugs.

That's it. We do **not** collect:

- Your name, email, address, phone number, or any account information (the app has no accounts)
- Your location
- Your photos, contacts, calendar, or microphone data
- The text of any custom activities you add (those stay on your device)
- Your Apple ID or device advertising identifier (IDFA)

## How data is collected

The app uses two Google Firebase services for the categories above:

- **Firebase Analytics** for usage events
- **Firebase Crashlytics** for crash and performance data

Firebase assigns a randomly-generated installation ID per device. This ID is not linked to any personal information and is reset if you reinstall the app. You can read Firebase's own privacy notice at <https://firebase.google.com/support/privacy>.

## How long data is kept

Firebase Analytics retains anonymous events for up to 14 months by default. Crash reports are kept for 90 days. We do not export or sell this data.

## Tracking

We do **not** use the Apple advertising identifier (IDFA), do not show any ads, and do not track you across other apps or websites. The app does not appear in Apple's "Allow [App] to track your activity?" prompt because nothing it does qualifies as tracking under Apple's definition.

## Children's privacy

The app is designed for parents and caregivers to find activities to do with their kids. It does not collect any personal information from anyone, including children. Custom activities you add are stored only on your device and are never transmitted off it.

If you believe a child has somehow provided personal information through the app, please contact us at the email below and we will delete any data we can identify.

## Your rights

Because we do not collect personal information, there is no account or profile to access, correct, or delete. If you want all anonymous analytics for your device removed, the simplest way is to delete the app — Firebase removes data tied to that installation ID after a short period.

If you are in the EU, UK, or California and want to make a formal request, email us and we will do our best to help, even though we generally have no identifiable data to act on.

## Changes to this policy

If we change what data the app collects, we will update this page and the "Last updated" date above. For material changes (e.g. adding new types of data collection), we will also note the change in the next app update's release notes.
